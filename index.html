Biography

Vasilis Bardakis is a fifth-year undergraduate student at the Department of Computer Engineering and Informatics (CEID) with a strong academic record, maintaining a GPA above 7.4. His passion for programming, which began at a young age, has led to excellence in software development courses and practical applications.

Currently, he is completing his thesis on developing dynamic ecosystems in Unity, scheduled for April 2024. His recent professional experience includes a year-long role (2023-2024) as a programmer in the funded university research project "3DSustainAvio," where he utilized Unity and VR technology to create immersive educational experiences for schools.
His technical expertise spans multiple programming languages and frameworks, including C, C++, C#, Java, Python, SQL, HTML, CSS, NextJS, and JavaScript. Beyond his academic and professional work, his experience in an auto repair shop and various woodworking projects has cultivated practical problem-solving skills and a hands-on approach to learning.
Fluent in English, Vasilis combines strong technical capabilities with excellent interpersonal skills, demonstrated through successful team collaborations in both university projects and team sports. His diverse background has fostered a self-confident, enthusiastic approach and an inherent drive to deeply understand and master new challenges.
